STRATEGOS — GAME TERMS GLOSSARY
================================

MATCH PHASES
------------
Token         Both players agree on a shared token budget using +/- buttons.
Selection     Tokens are the currency used to buy units in the next phase.
              Higher budget = bigger armies. Sets the scale of the match.

Character     Players take turns spending tokens to build their army. Buy L1
Selection     units for 1 token, upgrade L1->L2 for +2 tokens (3 total), and
              L2->L3 for +3 tokens (6 total). Any mix within budget. Players
              shop one at a time so neither sees the other's picks.

Character     Players take turns placing purchased units onto the board in
Placement     their starting zone (P1 rows 0-1, P2 rows 4-5). Placement is
              hidden — you can't see where your opponent puts their units
              until the planning phase begins.

Planning      Each player gives orders to every unit: move to a tile, then
Phase         choose an attack direction (or lend aid, plant a trap, channel,
              go stealth, etc. depending on unit type). Players plan one at
              a time with handoff screens so neither sees the other's moves.
              All decisions are locked in before the action phase begins.

Action        Orders execute simultaneously and visibly for both players.
Phase         Movement resolves first (charges, collisions, bounces, traps
              detonating), then attacks fire (arrows, firebolts, melee
              strikes), then damage resolves (kills, squeezes). This is the
              payoff — both players watch their plans collide in real time.


STATUS EFFECTS
--------------
Attrition     After a configurable turn threshold (default turn 12), every
              unit on the board loses 25% of its max HP per turn (rounded
              up). Prevents stalemates by forcing engagements. Can be
              disabled by setting the threshold to 0.

Channeling    A turn spent charging a powerful ability (Archmage). Unit can't
              attack. Visible to opponent as a warning. Can be interrupted by
              taking damage.

Exhausted     State after using a powerful ability (Archmage post-Fire Rain).
              Can't attack, possibly movement-restricted. Lasts one turn.

Inspired      Status granted when a Paladin charges through a friendly unit
              that is under its aura. The inspired unit's next attack deals
              double damage. Assassin variant: grants free stealth instead of
              double damage.

Stealth       Assassin becomes invisible to opponent. Costs a full turn to
              activate (unless inspired). Stealthed unit can shadow strike.


ACTIONS
-------
Shadow Strike Assassin's assassination attempt from stealth. Picks a target
              tile + attack direction. Three outcomes:
                - Enemy facing away: INSTANT KILL (perfect backstab)
                - Enemy facing sideways: enhanced arrow, Assassin revealed
                - Enemy facing toward Assassin: takes hit, no damage, revealed

Charge        Knight/Paladin 2-tile straight-line move (orthogonal only).
              Attacks into tile 3 for double damage (forward) or normal
              damage (sideways). Passes through friendly Archers/Squires
              (sidestep), tramples lighter enemies. Charging grants weight
              advantage in contested tiles.

Trample       When a charging Knight/Paladin passes through a lighter enemy
              unit. Deals partial damage without stopping.

Sidestep      Friendly Archers and Squires dodge out of a Knight/Paladin's
              charge path. They've trained together and know to move aside.

Cleave        Knight and Paladin sword-arc that hits tiles adjacent to the
              main attack. Pattern: the main attack strikes one orthogonal
              square, then cleave hits the 2 tiles flanking the target and
              the 2 tiles flanking the attacker (like a wide sword swing).
              The 3 squares behind the attacker are never hit. Cleave does
              NOT damage friendly units.
                - Knight:  cleave hits 1 best adjacent enemy
                - Paladin: cleave hits ALL enemies in the 4-tile arc

Lend Aid      Knight/Paladin heals an adjacent friendly unit 1 HP instead of
              attacking. Selected via green button during 1-tile move. Range 1.

Fire Rain     Archmage's channeled AOE attack. Massive magic damage over a
              target area. Requires a channel turn first, causes exhaustion
              after firing. Direct counter to clustered formations.

Trap          Rogue places an invisible explosive on a tile during planning.
              Triggers when any unit (friend or foe) steps on it. Full damage
              on the trigger tile, half damage on surrounding tiles. Rogue is
              immune to own trap's AOE. One trap per Rogue at a time. Fire
              (firebolt) passing over a trap detonates it.


MECHANICS
---------
Aura          Paladin's passive defensive bonus radiating 1 tile in all
              directions. Affects all friendly units in range. Enables the
              inspire combo when Paladin charges through an aura-buffed ally.

Contesting    A unit can move to a tile occupied by an enemy. If the enemy
              doesn't move, the defender has advantage (see Defender Holds).
              If both units move to the same empty tile, Tile Superiority
              resolves who lands it.

Tile          The order of tiebreakers used when two or more units want the
Superiority   same destination tile:
                1. MOMENTUM   — a charger always beats a non-charger
                2. WEIGHT     — heavier unit wins among equals on momentum
                3. REMAINING  — higher current HP wins among equals on weight
                   LIFE
              Only when all three are equal do BOTH units bounce. The loser
              is shoved back to their origin (or pushed off, see Bump).

Defender      When a unit advances into a tile where an enemy is stationary
Holds         (didn't move), the advancing unit automatically bounces UNLESS
              it is a Knight or Paladin. Knights and Paladins are heavy
              enough to contest a defender — normal weight check applies.

Bump          When a Knight/Paladin wins a weight check against a stationary
              defender, the defender is pushed off their tile. Push priority:
                1. Opposite direction from the attacker's approach
                2. Toward the defender's own end of the board (unoccupied)
                3. Toward the opponent's end of the board (unoccupied)
                4. If ALL surrounding tiles are occupied: SQUEEZE (eliminated)

Bounce        When a unit loses a weight contest or fails to push a
              stationary defender. The unit moves toward the tile, shakes,
              and slides back to its origin.

Squeeze       A bounced unit is eliminated if its origin tile is now occupied
              by a friendly unit. Nowhere to bounce back to.

Weight        Per-unit stat used as the SECOND tiebreaker for Tile
              Superiority (after Momentum). Higher weight wins.
              Squire/Knight/Paladin = 3, Adept/Mage/Archmage = 2,
              Archer/Rogue/Assassin = 1.

Momentum      The FIRST tiebreaker for Tile Superiority. A unit completing
              a charge (currently only Knight/Paladin via 2-square straight
              move) is "charging" — momentum 1. Anyone walking is
              momentum 0. Treated as a binary flag today; future units may
              earn momentum through other actions (sprint, dive, etc.) and
              may carry numeric momentum values to compare chargers
              against each other.

Remaining     The THIRD tiebreaker for Tile Superiority — current HP. Used
Life          to break weight ties so a wounded heavy can be shouldered
              aside by a fresh one of the same class.

Mutual        If both armies are completely eliminated in the same turn
Annihilation  (e.g. from attrition or simultaneous kills), the match is a
              draw. Neither player wins.


DAMAGE TYPES
------------
Piercing      Arrow damage. Dealt by Archer, Rogue, and Assassin ranged
              attacks. Fired orthogonally and diagonally.

Melee         Physical strike damage. Dealt by Squire, Knight, and Paladin.
              Orthogonal targets only (except cleave can hit diagonals).
              Knights and Paladins cannot move diagonally.

Explosive     Blast damage. Dealt by Rogue traps. Full on trigger tile, half
              on surrounding tiles. No other source yet.

Magic         Arcane damage. Dealt by Adept/Mage firebolt and Archmage fire
              rain. Firebolt hits all units in path (friendly fire possible).
              Magic users can move and attack diagonally.


CLASS TREES
-----------
Ranged:   Archer (L1)  ->  Rogue (L2)    ->  Assassin (L3)
Melee:    Squire (L1)  ->  Knight (L2)   ->  Paladin (L3)
Magic:    Adept (L1)   ->  Mage (L2)     ->  Archmage (L3)

Theme - Ranged tree:  Hidden information (arrows, traps, stealth)
Theme - Melee tree:   Frontline power + support (block, charge, aura, cleave)
Theme - Magic tree:   Escalating destruction (bolt, line AOE, area AOE)

Cost to field:  L1 = 1 token  |  L2 = 3 tokens  |  L3 = 6 tokens


DAMAGE FORMULA
--------------
Damage = Attack - Defense (minimum 1)
Charge = (Attack - Defense) x 2
Inspired = (Attack - Defense) x 2
Trap AOE (adjacent tiles) = (Attack - Defense) / 2
Cleave = same damage formula as main attack

Every hit always does at least 1 damage.
